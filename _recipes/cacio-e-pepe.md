title: "Cacio e Pepe"
description: "Rome's greatest contribution to pasta — three ingredients, one technique, infinite satisfaction."
category: Pasta
tags: [Italian, Vegetarian, Quick]
prep_time: 5 min
cook_time: 20 min
servings: 2
difficulty: Medium
image: /assets/images/cacio-e-pepe.jpg

ingredients:
  - 200g spaghetti or tonnarelli
  - 80g Pecorino Romano, finely grated
  - 30g Parmigiano Reggiano, finely grated
  - 2 tsp whole black peppercorns
  - Kosher salt, for pasta water
---

1. Toast the peppercorns in a dry skillet over medium heat for 2–3 minutes until fragrant. Crack coarsely in a mortar or with a heavy pan — you want a mix of powder and shards, not uniform dust.

1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Salt it lightly — less than you normally would, since Pecorino is very salty. Cook pasta 2 minutes shy of al dente.

1. While the pasta cooks, mix the two grated cheeses together in a bowl. Add 2–3 tbsp of cold water, a little at a time, stirring until you have a smooth, thick paste. It should look like pale putty.

1. Toast the cracked pepper in the same skillet over medium heat for 30 seconds. Add a ladle (about 80ml) of pasta cooking water. Let it simmer for 1 minute to infuse.

1. Drain the pasta, reserving a full cup of pasta water. Add pasta to the pepper skillet. Toss vigorously over medium-low heat, adding pasta water a splash at a time, until the pasta is glossy and almost al dente.

1. Remove from heat completely. Wait 30 seconds — this is important. Then add the cheese paste, tossing quickly and adding tiny splashes of pasta water to loosen until you have a creamy, cohesive sauce that clings to every strand. The off-heat step prevents the cheese from clumping.

1. Plate immediately. Finish with extra cracked pepper. Serve at once.
