title: "Brown Butter Chocolate Chip Cookies"
description: "Crispy edges, chewy centers, and a nutty caramel depth from browned butter. Better than the classic."
category: Baking
tags: [Cookies, Chocolate, Dessert]
prep_time: 20 min
cook_time: 12 min
servings: 18 cookies
difficulty: Easy
image: /assets/images/chocolate-chip-cookies.jpg

ingredients:
  - 225g unsalted butter
  - 200g light brown sugar, packed
  - 100g granulated sugar
  - 2 large eggs + 1 yolk
  - 2 tsp vanilla extract
  - 280g all-purpose flour
  - 1 tsp baking soda
  - 1 tsp fine salt
  - 300g dark chocolate (70%), roughly chopped
  - Flaky salt, to finish
---

1. Brown the butter: melt it in a light-colored saucepan over medium heat, swirling occasionally. It will foam, then subside, then smell like toffee. Pull it off the heat when the milk solids at the bottom turn golden brown, about 8 minutes. Pour into a large mixing bowl, scraping in every last bit. Let cool 10 minutes.

1. Whisk both sugars into the warm brown butter until combined. Add the eggs, extra yolk, and vanilla. Whisk vigorously for 2 full minutes — the mixture should become thick and ribbony. This step dissolves the sugar and builds structure.

1. Fold in the flour, baking soda, and salt with a spatula until just combined — no dry streaks, but don't overwork it. Fold in the chopped chocolate.

1. Refrigerate the dough for at least 1 hour, ideally overnight. Cold dough spreads less and develops more flavor.

1. When ready to bake, heat oven to 190°C (375°F). Line two baking sheets with parchment.

1. Scoop the dough into balls roughly 50g each (about 2 tablespoons). Space them 3 inches apart. Sprinkle generously with flaky salt.

1. Bake 11–13 minutes, rotating the pan halfway through, until the edges are set and golden but the centers still look underdone and glossy. They firm up as they cool.

1. Let cool on the pan for 5 minutes before moving. If you can wait 10 minutes to eat one, the texture will be at its best.
