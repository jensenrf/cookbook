servings: 4
difficulty: Easy

ingredients:
  - 1 whole chicken, 1.5–1.8kg
  - 1¼ tsp kosher salt (or ¾ tsp fine salt)
  - ½ tsp black pepper
  - 1 tbsp neutral oil or softened butter
  - 1 lemon, halved
  - 4 garlic cloves, unpeeled
  - Fresh thyme or rosemary (optional)
---

1. The night before (or at least 1 hour ahead): pat the chicken completely dry with paper towels — inside the cavity and all over the skin. Mix the salt and pepper and sprinkle it evenly over the entire bird, including the underside and inside the cavity. Place uncovered on a rack in the fridge overnight. Dry skin = crisp skin.

1. Remove the chicken from the fridge 30–45 minutes before cooking. A cold bird cooks unevenly.

1. Heat your oven to 230°C (450°F) with a rack in the upper-middle position. Place a cast-iron skillet or heavy roasting pan in the oven while it preheats.

1. Rub the chicken all over with oil or softened butter. Stuff the cavity loosely with the lemon halves, garlic, and herbs if using. Don't pack it tight.

1. Carefully place the chicken breast-side up in the preheated pan. The sizzle when it hits is the sound of good skin forming.

1. Roast for 45–55 minutes without opening the oven. The chicken is done when the thickest part of the thigh reads 74°C (165°F) on an instant-read thermometer, and the juices run clear when you pierce the thigh joint.

1. Rest the chicken on a cutting board, loosely tented with foil, for at least 10 minutes. Don't skip this — the juices redistribute and the meat finishes cooking through. Cut and serve.

1. Save the carcass. Simmer it with an onion, carrot, and celery for 2 hours and you have stock. Nothing wasted.
